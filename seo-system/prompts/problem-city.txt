CONTENT INSTRUCTIONS:

1. SLUG
Format: {problem-slug}-{city-slug}
Lowercase. Hyphens. No spaces.
Example: fireplace-wont-light-phoenix

2. TITLE (H1)
Name the problem + city. Clear and high-intent.
Example: "Gas Fireplace Won't Light in Phoenix, AZ"

3. META TITLE
Problem + city + trust/urgency signal. Max 60 characters.
Example: "Gas Fireplace Won't Light? Phoenix Repair - Same Day"

4. META DESCRIPTION
- 140-160 characters
- Acknowledge the problem + offer fast local help + CTA

5. INTRO (150-200 words)
- Open by naming the EXACT problem the homeowner is experiencing
- Paint the specific moment (e.g., "You flipped the switch, heard the
  click, and... nothing")
- Acknowledge it feels stressful/urgent without being alarmist
- Briefly hint at the 2-3 most likely causes
- Reassure them they're in the right place and we see this exact
  problem constantly in {{city}}
- Weave in {{city}} 1-2 times naturally
- No generic openings

6. LOCAL SECTION (150-200 words)
Why this specific problem shows up in {{city}}:
- desert heat / dust infiltration during the long off-season
- monsoon moisture damage
- seasonal usage patterns (many months of zero use)
- older homes vs newer builds in this area
- paraphrase FIELD NOTES naturally if relevant
Make it feel like: "we see this EXACT issue in {{city}} all the time."

7. CAUSES SECTION (220-280 words)
The meat of the diagnostic.

Open with one short paragraph framing the top 3-5 most common causes
for this problem. Then render a <ul> with 5-7 items where each item is:
- Cause name (bold-feeling, like "Failed thermocouple or thermopile")
- Followed by a 15-30 word plain-English explanation of what's happening
  and why it causes the symptom

This section is where homeowners decide if we actually know what we're
doing — be specific, technical, but readable. Talk about the part names
real pros use (pilot assembly, thermocouple, thermopile, gas valve,
control board, igniter, etc.).

Close with one short paragraph: "Without diagnostic tools it's hard to
know which cause is yours. That's what our inspection pins down."

8. DANGER SECTION (140-190 words)
When is this problem SAFE TO WAIT on, and when is it a CALL-NOW situation?

Use two clearly separated parts:
- "Safe to wait" — list 2-3 scenarios in a <ul> (e.g., "If the fireplace
  simply won't start and there's no gas smell, it can wait a day or two")
- "Call now" — list 3-4 red flags in a <ul> (gas smell, soot on wall,
  CO alarm sounding, visible flame outside the firebox, repeated tripping
  of the safety valve)

Be honest — do NOT make everything a "call now" emergency. That destroys trust.

9. DIY CHECKLIST SECTION (150-200 words)
Things a homeowner can safely try themselves before calling a pro.
Render as a short <ol> with 3-5 steps. Typical items:
- Verify the gas is on / the breaker isn't tripped
- Check the batteries in the remote or wall switch
- Look for a tripped ESO / safety switch
- Confirm the pilot is lit (if applicable)
- Clear any dust/lint visible around the pilot

For EACH step: one sentence of instruction + one sentence of what to
watch out for. Never recommend anything that involves opening a gas
valve, touching gas lines, or lighting pilots if unfamiliar.

Close with: "If you've tried these and the problem persists, call us —
we'll have a tech out the same day in {{city}}."

10. PROCESS SECTION (180-230 words)
How WE solve this specific problem when we arrive.
Open with a short paragraph framing our approach. Then render an <ol>
with 5-6 steps. Each step is: short name + 15-30 word explanation.

Typical flow for problem diagnosis:
1. Safety check on arrival (gas leak test, CO check)
2. Visual inspection of firebox, venting, pilot assembly
3. Diagnostic testing (combustion analyzer, manometer, multimeter)
4. Diagnosis explained + written estimate before we touch anything
5. Repair + verification testing
6. Final walkthrough with the homeowner

Be specific. Name the actual tools. Real pros use manometers, combustion
analyzers, leak detectors — mentioning them builds credibility.

11. PRICING SECTION (150-190 words)
Honest price range for this specific problem in {{city}}:
- Lead with the range from {{price_range}}

- Close with what pushes price up or down (parts availability, access,
  age of unit, after-hours calls)
- Mention the $99 diagnostic fee if applicable, and that it applies
