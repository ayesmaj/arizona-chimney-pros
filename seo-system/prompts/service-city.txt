CONTENT INSTRUCTIONS:

1. SLUG
Format: {service-slug}-{city-slug}
Lowercase. Hyphens. No spaces.

2. TITLE (H1)
Clear, local, high-intent. Include the exact primary keyword.
Example: "Gas Fireplace Repair in Phoenix, AZ"

3. META TITLE
Include keyword + city + trust signal. Max 60 characters.
Example: "Gas Fireplace Repair Phoenix AZ | Same-Day Service"

4. META DESCRIPTION
- 140-160 characters
- Include urgency + trust + CTA + primary keyword
- Must read naturally — not keyword-stuffed

5. INTRO (180-230 words)
- Open by speaking directly to the homeowner's situation
- Paint a specific scenario (e.g., "You walked into the living room to light

- Acknowledge what they're likely feeling (frustrated, worried about gas,

- Transition into who we are and why we're the right call
- Weave in {{city}} 1-2 times naturally
- Include the primary keyword once, naturally
- NO generic openings ("Welcome to..." / "In today's world...")

6. LOCAL SECTION (200-260 words)
Explain WHY this service is different in THIS city:
- climate factors (desert heat, monsoon moisture, dust infiltration)
- home styles (older adobe vs new build, luxury estates vs tract homes,
  vacation homes vs primary residence)
- usage patterns (long off-season, spike in October-March)
- unique issues this city sees that others don't (e.g., elevation, HOA
  restrictions, specific neighborhood characteristics)
- paraphrase the FIELD NOTES above naturally into this flow
Make it feel like: "we actually work in this city every week and we
see THIS specific pattern." Mention {{city}} 2-3 times naturally.

7. SIGNS SECTION (180-230 words)

- Specific (not "something feels wrong")
- Observable by a homeowner (they can actually see/smell/hear it)
- Mapped to the fuel type and appliance type on this page
Close with one short paragraph about what to do if they see 2+ signs.

8. PROBLEMS WE FIX SECTION (180-220 words)
Open with one short paragraph introducing this as the list of specific

- "Gas smell near the unit — always a safety call"
- "Flame is yellow/sooty instead of blue"
- "Remote or wall switch stopped working"

Each line: name the problem in homeowner language, then a 3-8 word

captures long-tail problem searches.

9. PRICING SECTION (220-280 words)

- After the table, a short paragraph explaining what pushes price up or

1. Arrival + homeowner walkthrough
2. Visual + safety inspection
3. Diagnostic testing (name actual tests — combustion analyzer, manometer, etc.)
4. Diagnosis explained to homeowner + written estimate
5. Repair + verification
6. Final test, cleanup, walkthrough

For remodels/installs, adapt accordingly (site prep, delivery, install, finish, handoff).
